Court Personnel

The Role of Court Personnel

  1. The judge presides over the courtroom and ensures that the rules of court are followed on both sides.  The judge also decides on questions of evidence and the application of the rules of procedure.
  2. The prosecutor represents the state and the defense attorney represents the accused.  Their job is to work towards the best interest of whomever they represent.
  3. The court clerk is responsible for the administrative aspects of the trial. (administering oaths, taking charge of physical evidence)
  4. The bailiff or deputy is in charge of maintaining safety and security in the court and escorting the jury and in-custody defendants in and out of proceedings.
  5. The court reporter records everything that is said as part of the formal proceedings.
  6. Additionally, court interpreters may be present to assist with interpretation and translation services.
