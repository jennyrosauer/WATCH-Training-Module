Domestic Violence

Definition

A pattern of abusive and threatening behaviors that may include physical, emotional, economic and sexual violence as well as intimidation, isolation and coercion. The purpose of domestic violence is to establish and exert power and control over another; men most often use it against their intimate partners, such as current or former spouses, girlfriends, or dating partners.

- The Advocates for Human Rights

Domestic Abuse Statute

Statute 518B

Domestic abuse occurs to a family or household member if committed by a family or household member. Household members are defined as: 

Spouse, former spouse, parents and children, person related by blood, currently living together or have lived together in the past, have a child in common, or are in a significant or sexual relationship.

Domestic Assault by Strangulation

Statute 609.2247

“Strangulation” means intentionally impeding normal breathing or circulation of the blood by applying pressure to the throat or neck or by blocking the nose or mouth of another person.
