Order for Protection

Order for Protection

An order for protection (OFP) is a court order that tells an abuser to stop harming or threatening an individual.  They can also direct other behaviors, such as staying away from a victim, vacating the premises, and refraining from contacting the victim.

The Civil Order for Protection Process

  • Orders for protection are a civil order that, if broken, have criminal sanctions. If the respondent violates the order, he or she would be subject to arrest and charged with a Felony .
  • OFPS were designed specifically to deal with the complicated dynamics of domestic violence.

  • Orders for protection can be effective tools for some victims but cannot guarantee safety.  It is up to each individual to determine if requesting an OFP would help or hurt the situation.
