Prosecution and Pre-Trial Proceedings

First Appearance Proceedings

  • Arrest   ->   First appearance
  • Someone is arrested then brought before a judge for a first appearance.

In a First Appearance hearing:

  • A public defender may be appointed. 
  • Defendant enters plea.
  • Judge sets bail & conditions (if any).

Felony Cases

First appearance can be used as probable

cause hearing

  • The judge determines if there is enough evidence on behalf of the State to proceed.

Appearances

In general, if a case is going to trial, a variety of court hearings will be held to prepare for it.

Appearances may include: 

  • Motion hearings
  • Change of venue hearings
  • Hearings to determine the admissibility of evidence
  • Jury selection (“voir dire”)
