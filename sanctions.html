Sanctions

Types of Sanctions

  • Prison/jail time
    • May be stayed; meaning the defendant won’t go directly to jail. If they violate probation, they will be arrested and sent to prison to serve their time.
  • Probation 
    • Each defendant put on probation is assigned a probation officer to report to.
  • Community service
  • Restitution
    • A payment made on behalf of the defendant to the state, victim or victim’s family.
  • Treatment 
    • Domestic abuse counseling, drugs or alcohol treatment, anger management, etc...

Violations of Sanctions

Commonly referred to as probation violation

  • A hearing is scheduled 
  • May result in new charges and/or having “stayed” time revoked.
  • If defendant denies violation, revocation (Morrissey) hearing is scheduled.

Probation Violations

Courts most commonly learn about probation violations: 

  • From defendant’s probation officer
    • Violate condition of probation ordered at Sentencing
  • Arrested by authorities on another charge
    • Authorities may look up a defendant’s criminal history
  • Violated a No Contact Order or Order for Protection
    • Victim may call 911 to tell authorities defendant has tried to contact victim.
