Sexual Assault

Definition

“A nonconsensual sexual contact that is obtained through coercion or the use or threat of force. Sexual assault is not a manifestation of uncontrolled desire, attraction, or arousal; it  is a deliberate act of gender-based violence and an expression of power, control and domination over another.”

- The Advocates for Human Rights

Assault

Statutes 609.221 to 609.224

  • 1st degree – “great bodily harm”; or assault with deadly force against police officer
  • 2nd degree – “substantial bodily harm” and uses a dangerous weapon
  • 3rd degree – “substantial bodily harm”; or pattern of child abuse; or assaults a child under 4 years old and inflicts bodily harm
  • 4th degree – assault against a police officer, firefighter, emergency medical personnel, certain government/public/school employees; or assault motivated by race, gender, sexual orientation, or other bias. This charge is never “ours” meaning we do not monitor this case in court.
  • 5th degree – misdemeanor: an act committed with intent to cause fear of bodily harm or death; or intentionally inflicts or attempts to inflict bodily harm upon another.

Sex Trafficking in Minnesota

609.321 PROSTITUTION AND SEX TRAFFICKING; DEFINITIONS.

Subd. 7a.Sex trafficking.

"Sex trafficking" means:

(1) receiving, recruiting, enticing, harboring, providing, or obtaining by any means an individual to aid in the prostitution of the individual; or

(2) receiving profit or anything of value, knowing or having reason to know it is derived from an act described in clause (1).

Subd. 7b.Sex trafficking victim.

"Sex trafficking victim" means a person subjected to the practices in subdivision 7a.

Subd. 8.Prostitute.

"Prostitute" means an individual who engages in prostitution by being hired, offering to be hired, or agreeing to be hired by another individual to engage in sexual penetration or sexual contact.

Subd. 9.Prostitution.

"Prostitution" means hiring, offering to hire, or agreeing to hire another individual to engage in sexual penetration or sexual contact, or being hired, offering to be hired, or agreeing to be hired by another individual to engage in sexual penetration or sexual contact.


You can find the entire MN Statute here: Sex Trafficking Statutes in MN

Criminal Sexual Conduct (CSC)

Statutes 609.342 to 609.3451

  • 1st degree - requires penetration or contact with a child under 13 years and one of the following: armed with a weapon; exhibits force or coercion; offender knows victim is mentally impaired or incapacitated
  • 2nd degree - same as 1st degree except no penetration, only contact.
  • 3rd degree - requires penetration and has less of an age disparity than 1st degree. This degree includes sexual abuses by clergy, psychotherapist, etc.
  • 4th degree - same as 3rd degree except no penetration, only contact.
  • 5th degree - gross misdemeanor. Offender engages in nonconsensual sexual conduct contact (e.g. touching), or offender engages in public masturbation or lewd exhibition of genitals in front of minor under age 16.

Facts

  • In 2009 the Twin Cities was identified by the FBI as the second largest area for child prostitution in the Nation.
  • In 2009 the Minnesota laws on prostitution were amended to explicitly make sex trafficking a crime.
  • In 2011 Minnesota passed the Safe Harbor Laws.  
    • Part 1 of the law went into effect in 2011 and added the definition of sexually exploited youth in Minnesota’s child protection codes.
    • Part 2 of the law went into effect August 1st, 2014, mandating that anyone under the age of 18 who is a victim of sex trafficking cannot be treated as a criminal or be labeled as a “delinquent child.”

  • The Advocates became a leader in Minnesota’s anti-trafficking efforts. In 2011, The Advocates led the effort to enact Minnesota’s Safe Harbor for Sexually Exploited Youth Act. 

  • In late 2013, WATCH began prioritizing cases of sex trafficking as they moved slowly through the Hennepin County criminal justice system.  In 2013, WATCH committed to monitor all cases of sex trafficking in Hennepin County from start to finish.
