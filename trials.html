Trials

Burden of Proof

State has burden of proof.

  • The prosecutor must convince the jury or judge that the defendant is guilty beyond a reasonable doubt.

Trials

Unlike what you may see on television, most cases do not result in a trial.

Trials are expensive and time consuming, and there is often pressure for the parties to come to a settlement, known as a plea bargain, to resolve the case and avoid a trial.

Types of Trials

Jury trial- traditionally composed of 12 individuals who determine the guilt or innocence of the defendant.


Judge or bench trial- where a judge hears the evidence and makes the determination.
