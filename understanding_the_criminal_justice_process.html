Understanding the Criminal Justice Process

The Criminal Justice Process

Crimes are divided into three categories based on the maximum possible length of imprisonment:

  • Misdemeanor; punishable by 90 days or less.
  • Gross Misdemeanor; punishable by one year or less.
  • Felony; punishable by imprisonment of more than 1 year up to life in prison.

A Crime Takes Place

Crimes can be brought to the attention of the authorities in a variety of ways.

  • Child reports abuse to a parent or loved one
  • Victim may contact police by calling 911
  • Routine welfare checks 
  • Investigations of separate or related offenses

Incident    ->   Report   ->    Investigation   ->    Arrest

Charges are brought

Criminal activity is a crime against the state, not the individual. 

  • To say that a victim “brought charges” or “dropped a case” is incorrect.
