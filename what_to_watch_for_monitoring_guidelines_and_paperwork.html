What to watch for, Monitoring Guidelines, and Paperwork

Which Courtrooms will you be observing?

  • Hennepin County
    • Felony Arraignment Court (1st Appearances)
    • Domestic Violence Court 
    • Felony hearings at the Government Center
    • Order for Protection Court 
  • Ramsey County
    • Main Courthouse
  • Washington County
    • Main Courthouse

Volunteer Expectations

  • Briefing and debriefing
    • Communicate with The Advocates prior to every shift to discuss cases and the calendar.
    • Please bring homework or reading material for any downtime in the courtroom.
  • Proper court attire & prohibited items
    • Anything but torn jeans, sweatpants, flip flops or shirts with provocative words.  You will be required to enter each courthouse through metal detectors. Please prepare for each shift with this in mind.
  • Cancellations 
    • Please call The Advocates 24 hours in advance, or if sick please, e-mail as soon as possible.

Courtroom Guidelines

  • You are a silent observer of the system.
  • Please do not discuss the cases you see, give feedback to any court personnel or discuss cases with anyone sitting in the gallery.
  • Monitors should not INITIATE conversation or feedback with judges.  On occasion the judge or other courtroom personnel may engage in conversation with you, which is welcomed.

Jury Trials

  • We do not observe testimonies of victims under the age of 13.
    • This is out of respect and to reduce the trauma for both victims and monitors.
  • When attending trials please enter and exit quietly. 
  • If you recognize a jury member, please leave the trial immediately.
  • Please be aware of who is around you in the halls, elevators and security lines etc.  Information relevant to the case may be said outside of the courtroom.
  • Deputies are only present in the courtroom if a defendant is in custody.
  • If you are ever worried about safety, notify the judicial clerk or leave the courtroom immediately.  

What to Monitor

We monitor the process and substance of court proceedings.

Process:

Start times, conduct, audibility, victim/public safety, etc.

Substance:

Plea bargains, dispositions, departure from sentencing

guidelines, victim and defendant statements, victim

safety, pre-investigation, etc.

You are monitoring the ENTIRE process, including what happens before, during and after appearances, both in the gallery and in the attorney area. You may overhear interesting comments or feedback in the elevators or other areas adjacent to the courts.
